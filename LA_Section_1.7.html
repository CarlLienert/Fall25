Put the vector in as columns of a matrix, \(A\). If \(A{\bf x} ={\bf 0}\) has only the trivial solution, the vectors are linearly independent.

\(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & -3\\ 0 & 5 & 4\\ 2 & -8 & 1\end{bmatrix}\) reduces to

10.

\({\bf v_3} \in \text{Span} \{{\bf v_2}, {\bf v_1} \}\)

If \(x_1 {\bf v_1} + x_2 {\bf v_2}={\bf v_3}\) has a solution. To solve, consider \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 2\\ -5 & 10 & -10\\ -3 & 6 & h \end{bmatrix}\)

as an augmented matrix.

Reduce:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 2\\ -5 & 10 & -10\\ -3 & 6 & h \end{bmatrix}\)

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 2\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & h+6 \end{bmatrix}\)

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 2\\ 0 & 0 & h+6\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}\)

This system is consistent only if \(h=-6\). So, \({\bf v_3} \in \text{Span} \{{\bf v_2}, {\bf v_1} \}\) if \(h=-6\).

To determine if the three vectors are linearly independent consider the matrix

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 2\\ -5 & 10 & -10\\ -3 & 6 & h \end{bmatrix}\)

as the matrix \(A\) in the equation \(A {\bf x}={\bf 0}\)

Regardless of the value of \(h\) the matrix \(A\) has at least one free variable. (\(A\) has two free variables if \(h=-6\).)

So, the three vectors are linearly independent for all values of \(h\).

14.

Put the vectors as columns in a matrix \(A\) and reduce. The reduced matrix is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -5 & 1\\ 0 & 1 & 1\\ 0 & 0 & h-26 \end{bmatrix}\)

which has a free variable if \(h=26\). Thus the vectors are linearly dependent if \(h=26\). The vectors are linearly independent if \(h \neq 26\).

16.

The second vector is \(\frac{3}{2}\) times the first vector. Thus:

\(\frac{3}{2}{\bf v_1}={\bf v_2}\)

or

\(\frac{3}{2}{\bf v_1}-{\bf v_2}={\bf 0}\)

That is, the two vectors are linearly dependent.
