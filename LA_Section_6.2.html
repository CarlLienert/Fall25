10.

Check that \({\bf u}_1 \cdot {\bf u}_2=0\), \({\bf u}_2 \cdot {\bf u}_3=0\), and \({\bf u}_1 \cdot {\bf u}_3=0\). Since none of these vectors is the zero vector, this set of orthogonal vectors is linearly independent and hence forms a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

To write \({\bf x}\) as a linear combination of the basis vectors, compute:

\[\begin{align*} c_1&=\frac{{\bf x}\cdot {\bf u}_1}{{\bf u}_1\cdot {\bf u}_1}=\frac{15+9}{9+9}=\frac{24}{18}=\frac{4}{3}\\ \\ c_2&= \frac{{\bf x}\cdot {\bf u}_2}{{\bf u}_2\cdot {\bf u}_2}=\frac{10-6-1}{4+4+1}=\frac{3}{9}=\frac{1}{3}\\ \\ c_3&= \frac{{\bf x}\cdot {\bf u}_3}{{\bf u}_3\cdot {\bf u}_3}=\frac{5-3+4}{1+1+16}=\frac{6}{18}=\frac{1}{3}\\ \\ \end{align*}\]

So \({\bf x}=\frac{4}{3}{\bf u}_1+\frac{1}{3}{\bf u}_2+\frac{1}{3}{\bf u}_3.\)

\({\bf u}_\parallel=\begin{bmatrix}3\\6\end{bmatrix}\). Then \({\bf u}\perp={\bf y}-{\bf u}_\parallel=\begin{bmatrix}-6\\3\end{bmatrix}\) whose magnitude is \(\sqrt{36+9}=\sqrt{45}=3\sqrt{5}\) which is the distance from \({\bf y}\) to the line through \({\bf u}\).

20.

The two vectors are orthogonal. The magnitude of the first vector is \(\sqrt{\frac{4}{9}+\frac{4}{9}+\frac{1}{9}}=1\) so it is a unit vector. The magnitude of the second vector is \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{9}+\frac{4}{9}}=\frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}\) so the second vector is not a unit vector. It can be normalized by scaling by \(\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}}\) to obtain \(\begin{bmatrix}\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\\\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{bmatrix}.\) Now the original first vector and the normalized second vector are an orthonormal set.
