26.

We need to check that the zero signal is in \(W\) and that signals in \(W\) are closed under addition and scalar multiplication.

Let \(r=0\) to get the zero signal.

If two signals in \(W\) are:

\((\dots, 0,0, r, r, r, \dots)\) and \((\dots, 0, 0, s, s, s, \dots)\) then the sum of the two signals is \((\dots, 0,0,r+s, r+s, r+s, \dots)\) which is in \(W\) so \(W\) is closed under signal addition.

A scalar, \(k\), times the first signal above is \((\dots, 0, 0, kr, kr, kr, \dots)\) which is in \(W\) so \(W\) is closed under scalar multiplication.
