12. \({\bf b}\) is a linear combination of \({\bf a_1}\), \({\bf a_2}\), and \({\bf a_3}\) if the vector equation \[x_1 {\bf a_1}+x_2{\bf a_2}+x_3{\bf a_3}={\bf b}\] has a solution. We determine this by considering the associated augmented matrix and reducing. Notice: We don’t need reduced echelon form to answer the question! Echelon form will suffice to know if the system is consistent.

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & -5\\ -2 & 5 & 0 & 11\\ 2 & 5 & 8 & -7 \end{bmatrix}\]

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & -5\\ 0 & 5 & 4 & 1\\ 0 & 5 & 4 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\]

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & -5\\ 0 & 5 & 4 & 1\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\]

The bottom row represents: \(0x_1+0x_2+0x_3=2\), or \(0=2\). So, this system is inconsistent, there is no solution to the vector equation, \({\bf b}\) is NOT a linear combination of \({\bf a_1}\), \({\bf a_2}\), and \({\bf a_3}\).

18. We need to know if the vector equation \[x_1{\bf v_1}+x_2{\bf v_2}={\bf y}\] has a solution.

The associated augmented matrix is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & h\\ 0 & 1 & -5\\ -2 & 8 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\)

We’ll reduce only enough to be able to answer the question whether the system is consistent or not.

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & h\\ 0 & 1 & -5\\ 0 & 2 & -3+2h \end{bmatrix}\]

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & h\\ 0 & 1 & -5\\ 0 & 0 & 7+2h \end{bmatrix}\]

For this system to be consistent we need \(7+2h=0\), \(h=-\frac{7}{2}\).

The vector \({\bf y}\) is in the plane generated by \({\bf v_1}\) and \({\bf v_2}\) only if \(h=-\frac{7}{2}\).
