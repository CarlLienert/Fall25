6.

The eigenvalues are \(\lambda=3\), and \(\lambda=4\).

For \(\lambda=3\) a basis for the 2-dimensional eigenspace space is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}2\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-1\\-1\\0\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).

For \(\lambda=4\) a basis for the 1-dimensional eigenspace is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}-2\\-1\\0\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).

10.

The characteristic equation is \[(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)-12=\lambda^2-3\lambda-10=(\lambda-5)(\lambda+2)\] so the eigenvalues are \(5\) and \(-2\).

For \(\lambda=5\), \[A-5I=\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 3\\4 & -4\end{bmatrix}\sim\begin{bmatrix}1 & -1 \\0&0\end{bmatrix}\] so an eigenvector is \(\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}\)

For \(\lambda=-2\), \[A+2I=\begin{bmatrix}4 & 3\\4 & 3\end{bmatrix}\sim\begin{bmatrix}4 & 3 \\0&0\end{bmatrix}\] and an eigenvector is \(\begin{bmatrix}3\\-4\end{bmatrix}\)

The diagonalization is given by \(P=\begin{bmatrix}1&3\\ 1&-4\end{bmatrix}\) and \(D=\begin{bmatrix}5&0\\0&-2\end{bmatrix}\).

14.

For \(\lambda=3\):

\[A-\lambda I=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 2\\ 2 & 0 & 4\\0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\sim \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 2\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\] and a the general solution to \((A-\lambda I){\bf x}={\bf 0}\) is \[x_2\begin{bmatrix}0\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}+x_3\begin{bmatrix}-2\\0\\1\end{bmatrix}\]. Take the two vectors that appear in the general solution as basis vectors for this eigenspace.

For \(\lambda=4\): \[A-\lambda I=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 2\\ 2 & -1 & 4\\0 & 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\sim \begin{bmatrix}1 & -\frac{1}{2} & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}.\] Solve the homogeneous equation \((A-\lambda I){\bf x}={\bf 0}\) to find a basis for the eigenspace: \(\begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\0\end{bmatrix}\).

Finally:

\(P=\begin{bmatrix}0&-2&1\\ 1&0&2\\ 0&1&0\end{bmatrix}\) and \(D=\begin{bmatrix}3&0&0\\0&3&0\\0&0&4\end{bmatrix}\).
