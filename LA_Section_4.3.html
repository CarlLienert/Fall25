8.

Put the vectors in as columns to a matrix \(A\). Row reduce. You’l find there are 3 pivots, one in every row. Thus the set of 4 vectors span \(\mathbb{R}^3\). However, the equation \(A{\bf x}={\bf 0}\) has non-trivial solutions (not a pivot in every column) and so the set of 4 vectors is not linearly independent.

10.

Reduce the matrix:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -5 & 0 & 7\\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 0 & 6\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\)

Write down the general solution. The vectors in this general solution give a basis for the null space:

\[\left\{\begin{bmatrix}5\\4\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-7\\-6\\0\\3\\1 \end{bmatrix}\right\}\]

14.

A basis for \(\text{Col }A\) is formed by the pivot columns from \(A\):

\(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\1\\3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}-5\\-5\\0\\-5\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-3\\2\\5\\-2\end{bmatrix} \right\}\)

A basis for \(\text{Row }A\) is formed by the non-zero rows of \(B\):

\(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1&2&0&4&5\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}0&0&5&-7&8\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}0&0&0&0&-9\end{bmatrix}\right\}\)

To find a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\), first complete the row reduction to reduced row echelon form. Then write the general solution. A basis is then:

\(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}-2\\1\\0\\0\\0\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}-4\\0\\\frac{7}{5}\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\right\}\)

16.

Put the vectors as columns in a matrix \(A\). Row reduce:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1&0&0&-1&-2\\ 0&1&0&-3&5\\ 0&0&1&0&2\\ 0&0&0&0&0\end{bmatrix}\)

The first three columns have pivots. Take the first three columns from \(A\) (the first three vectors in the list) as the basis. Make sure to take them from \(A\), NOT the reduced matrix.

20.

Since \({\bf v_1}\) can be written as a linear combination of \({\bf v_2}\) and \({\bf v_3}\) remove \({\bf v_1}\) from the set. Then verify that \({\bf v_2}\) is not a scalar multiple of \({\bf v_3}\). A basis is then \(\left\{{\bf v_2}, {\bf v_3}\right\}\)

In fact, any two of the vectors form a basis. Removing \({\bf v_1}\) is just the easiest to see.
