Since the 4 vectors form an orthogonal basis for \(\mathbb{R}^4\) we’ll find a projection onto \(\text{span}\{{\bf u}_1\}\). Subtraction will yield the other vector.

\(\text{proj}_{{\bf u}_1}{\bf v}=\frac{{\bf y}\cdot{\bf u}_1}{{\bf u}_1\cdot {\bf u_1}}{\bf u}_1=\frac{14}{7}\begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}2\\4\\2\\2\end{bmatrix}\)

The vector in \(\text{span}\left\{{\bf u}_2, {\bf u}_3, {\bf u}_4\right\}\) is \[{\bf v}-\text{proj}_{{\bf u}_1}=\begin{bmatrix}4\\5\\-2\\2\end{bmatrix}-\begin{bmatrix}2\\4\\2\\2\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}2\\1\\-4\\0\end{bmatrix}.\]
