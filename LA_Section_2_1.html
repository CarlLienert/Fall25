30.

If the columns of \(B\) are linearly dependent, then the matrix equation \(B{\bf x}={\bf 0}\) has a non-trivial solution. Call one such non-trivial solution \({\bf \hat{x}}\) (so \({\bf \hat{x}} \neq {\bf 0}\)). Then \[AB{\bf \hat{x}}=A\left( B{\bf \hat{x}}\right)=A{\bf 0}={\bf 0}.\]

That is, \({\bf \hat{x}}\) is also a non-trivial solution for the equation \(AB{\bf x}={\bf 0}\) and so the columns of \(AB\) form a linearly independent set.

32.

Suppose \(AD=I_m\). Let \({\bf b} \in \mathbb{R}^m\). Multiply the matrix identity by \({\bf b}\) on the right:

\[AD{\bf b}=I_m {\bf b}={\bf b}\]

A solution to \(A{\bf x}={\bf b}\) is \({\bf x}=D{\bf b}.\)

The matrix \(A\) has dimension \(m \times n\) for some \(n\). (\(m\) is the same \(m\) as that of \(I_m\).) so \({\bf x} \mapsto A{\bf x}\) is onto since \(A{\bf x}={\bf b}\) has a solution for all \(b \in \mathbb{R}^m\). \(A\) must have a pivot in every row. If \(A\) had more rows than columns this would not be possible.

35.

\({\bf u}^T{\bf v}=-2a+3b-4c\) (this is the dot or inner product) of \({\bf u}\) and \({\bf v}\)

\({\bf v}^T {\bf u}\) is the same thing as above. You should write down the vectors to see how they present differently, but give the same result.

\({\bf u}{\bf v}^T=\begin{bmatrix}-2\\3\\-4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}a & b & c\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-2a & -2b & -2c\\ 3a & 3b & 3c\\ -4a & -4b & -4c\end{bmatrix}\)

\({\bf v}{\bf u}^T=\begin{bmatrix}-2a & 3a & -4a\\ -2b & 3b & -4b\\ -2c & 3c & -4c\end{bmatrix}\)

Notice \({\bf u}{\bf v}^T\) and \({\bf v}{\bf u}^T\) are NOT the same.

The easiest way to make sure you have the “right” thing is to write the appropriate vectors next to each other and think about the row-column method of computing matrix multiplication.
