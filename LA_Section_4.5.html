6.

The subspace is spanned by:

\(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3\\6\\-9\\-3\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}6\\-2\\5\\1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-1\\-2\\3\\1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\)

Notice that the first vector is \(-3\) times the last vector. So, remove the first vector. (Or, the last vector, it doesn’t matter.)

The second and third vectors are not scalar multiplies of each other so they are linearly independent.

A basis consists of the second two vectors, and the dimension is 2.

27.

The vector representation of these polynomials as columns in a matrix is:

\(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1&0&-2&0\\ 0 & 2 & 0 &-12\\ 0 & 0 & 4 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 8\end{bmatrix}.\)

No row reduction is needed: you can see there are 4 pivots. This would reduce to the identity matrix. The equation \(A{\bf x}={\bf 0}\) has only the trivial solution. Hence the polynomials (vectors) are linearly independent. Also, they span \(\mathbb{P}_3\) since the dimension of the space is 4.

32.

A basis for \(H\) consists of \(n\) vectors. These vectors are also in \(V\). They are linearly independent regardless of whether you think of them as vectors of \(H\) or \(V\). \(n\) linearly independent vector in \(V\) form a basis for \(V\). Any vector in \(V\) can be written as a linear combination of the basis vectors for \(H\). So, \(H\)=\(V\).
