6.

Solve \(A^TA{\bf x}=A^T{\bf b}\).

\(A^TA=\begin{bmatrix}6&3&3\\ 3&3&0\\ 3&0&3\end{bmatrix}\)

\(A^T{\bf b}=\begin{bmatrix}27\\11\\15\end{bmatrix}\)

Row reduce the augmented matrix to obtain: \[\begin{bmatrix}1&0&1&5\\ 0&1&1&-1\\ 0&0&0&0\end{bmatrix}\]

So the general solution is \[{\bf \hat{x}}=\begin{bmatrix}5\\-1\\0\end{bmatrix}+x_3\begin{bmatrix}-1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}.\]

These are the least squares solutions.

12.

The columns of \(A\) are orthogonal so the projection of \({\bf b}\) onto the column space of \(A\) is:

\[\begin{align*} &\frac{2+5-6}{3}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\-1\end{bmatrix}+\frac{2+6+6}{3}\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}+\frac{-5+6-6}{3}\begin{bmatrix}0\\-1\\1\\-1\end{bmatrix}\\ &= \frac{1}{3}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\-1\end{bmatrix}+\frac{14}{3}\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}+-\frac{5}{3}\begin{bmatrix}0\\-1\\1\\-1\end{bmatrix}\\ &=\begin{bmatrix}5\\2\\3\\6\end{bmatrix} \end{align*}\]

The least squares solution is the solution to \(A{\bf x}={\bf \hat{b}}\). From above the solution is: \({\bf \hat{x}}=\begin{bmatrix}1/3\\14/3\\-5/3\end{bmatrix}\)

The augmented matrix is:

\[\begin{bmatrix}2&3&17/2\\ 0&5&9/2\end{bmatrix}\]

Then \(2x_1+3\cdot \frac{9}{10}=\frac{17}{2}\) and \(x_1=\frac{29}{10}\). So \({\bf \hat{x}}=\begin{bmatrix}\frac{29}{10}\\ \\\frac{9}{10}\end{bmatrix}\)
