2.

\(\text{det}(A-\lambda I)=(-1-\lambda)(-5-\lambda)+5=\lambda^2+6\lambda+10\)

The roots of this polynomial are:

\[\lambda=\frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{36-4(1)(10)}}{2}=\frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{-4}}{2}=-3 \pm i\]

For \(\lambda=-3-i\):

\[A-\lambda I=\begin{bmatrix}2+i & -1\\ 5 & -2+i\end{bmatrix},\] an eigenvector is \(\begin{bmatrix}-1\\2+i\end{bmatrix}\).

For \(\lambda=-3+i\):

\[A-\lambda I =\begin{bmatrix}2-i& -1\\ 5& -2-i\end{bmatrix},\] an eigenvector is \(\begin{bmatrix}1\\2-i\end{bmatrix}\).

Each eigenvector forms a basis for the corresponding eigenspace.
