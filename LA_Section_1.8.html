6.

Use the augmented matrix:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1 & 1\\ 3 & -4 & 5 & 9\\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3\\ -3 & 5 & -4 & -6 \end{bmatrix}\)

which reduces to:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 3 & 7\\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}\)

The variable \(x_3\) is free. The reduced matrix gives:

\(x_1+3x_3=7\) and \(x_2+x_3=3\)

The general solution is then:

\({\bf x}=\begin{bmatrix}7-3x_3\\3-x_3\\x_3\end{bmatrix}\).

The question asks for one solution, so pick a value for \(x_3\). I’ll pick \(x_3=0\) and \(\begin{bmatrix}7\\3\\0\end{bmatrix}\) is a solution.

32.

Since \({\bf v_1}, {\bf v_2}, \dots, {\bf v_p}\) span \(\mathbb{R}^n\), \({\bf x}\) is a linear combination of the vectors: \[{\bf{x}}=x_1{\bf v_1}+x_2{\bf v_2}+\dots x_p{\bf v_p}\]

for some values of \(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_p\).

Then

\[T({\bf{x}})=x_1T({\bf v_1})+x_2T({\bf v_2})+\dots x_pT({\bf v_p})={\bf 0}\]

Since \(T({\bf v_i})={\bf 0}\) for each of the vectors \({\bf v_1}, {\bf v_2}, \dots, {\bf v_p}\).

38.

Consider the image of the sum of two vectors \({\bf u}\) and \({\bf v}\):

\[T({\bf u}+{\bf v})=A({\bf u}+{\bf v})+{\bf b}=A{\bf u} +A{\bf v}+{\bf b}\]

but

\[T({\bf u})+T({\bf v})=A{\bf u} +A{\bf v}\]

so

\(T({\bf u}+{\bf v})= T({\bf u})+T({\bf v})\) only if \({\bf b}={\bf 0}\).
