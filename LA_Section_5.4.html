4.

(a)

If \({\bf p}\) and \({\bf q}\) are polynomials in \(\mathbb{P}_2\), then:

\[\begin{align*} T({\bf p}+{\bf q})&=({\bf p+q})(0)-({\bf p+q})(1)t+({\bf p+q})(2)t^2\\ &={\bf p}(0)+{\bf q}(0)-{\bf p}(1)t-{\bf q}(1)t+{\bf p}(2)t^2+{\bf q}(2)t^2\\ &= \left[{\bf p}(0)-{\bf p}(1)t+{\bf p}(2)t^2\right]+\left[ {\bf q}(0) -{\bf q}(1)t +{\bf q}(2)t^2\right]\\ &=T({\bf p})+T({\bf q})\end{align*}\]

and for a scalar \(k\):

\[\begin{align*} T(k{\bf p})&= k{\bf p}(0)-k{\bf p}(1)t+k{\bf p}(2)t^2\\ &=k\left[{\bf p}(0)-{\bf p}(1)t+{\bf p}(2)t^2\right]\\ &=kT({\bf p}) \end{align*}\]

Hence, \(T\) is linear.

(b) If \({\bf p}(t)=-2+t\), then \({\bf p}(0)=-2\), \({\bf p}(1)=-1\), and \({\bf p}(2)=0\). So, \[T({\bf p})=-2+t.\]

Since \(T({\bf p})={\bf p}\), \({\bf p}\) is an eigenvector with eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\).

\[\begin{align*} T(1)&=1-t+t^2\\ T(t)&=-t+2t^2\\ T(t^2)&=-t+4t^2 \end{align*}\]

So, the matrix for \(T\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}1&0&0\\-1&-1&-1\\ 1&2&4\end{bmatrix}\)

8.

We compute \(P^{-1}AP\) where \(P\) contains the \(\mathcal{B}\) basis vectors:

\(\frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}1&1\\ -2 &3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 4\\ -2 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3 & -1\\ 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2\\-2 &1\end{bmatrix}\)

This is the matrix that sends \([{\bf x}]_\mathcal{B} \rightarrow [T({\bf x})]_\mathcal{B}\) where \(A\) is the matrix that sends \({\bf x} \rightarrow T({\bf x})\).

10.

We need a diagonal matrix \(D\) that is similar to \(A\). Remember, we can diagonalize \(A\) by finding the eigenvalues and eigenvectors. The eigenvectors are the columns of \(P\) and and the eigenvalues are the diagonal entries of \(D\). The entries of \(P\) form a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^2\).

The characteristic equation is \[(5-\lambda)(1-\lambda)-21=\lambda^2-6\lambda-16=(\lambda-8)(\lambda+2).\]

For \(\lambda_1=8\), \[A-8I= \begin{bmatrix}-3 & -3\\ -7 & -7\end{bmatrix}\] and an eigenvector is \({\bf x}_1=\begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\end{bmatrix}\).

For \(\lambda_2=-2\), \[A+2I=\begin{bmatrix}7 & -3\\-7 & 3\end{bmatrix}\] and an eigenvector is \({\bf x}_2=\begin{bmatrix}3\\7\end{bmatrix}\)

So a basis such that \([T]_\mathcal{B}\) is diagonal (and in fact contains the eigenvalues on the diagonal) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}3\\7\end{bmatrix}\right\}.\)
