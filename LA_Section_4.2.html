44.

Polynomials in \(\mathbb{P}_2\) are of the form \(p(t)=at^2+bt+c.\) We want to find those polynomials such that \(p(0)=0.\) They are the polynomials with \(c=0\). That is \(\text{ker } T=\{p(t): p(t)=at^2+bt\}.\) The polynomials \(p_1(t)=t\) and \(p_2(t)=t^2\) span this space.

Notice the codomain is usually known as the \(xy\) - plane. The range of \(T\) is the line \(y=x\) (or \(x_1=x_2\)) through the origin.
