6.

The reduced echelon form of the coefficient matrix is

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 4\\ 0 & 1 & -3\\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\)

This represents:

\(\begin{cases} x_1+4x_3=0\\ x_2-3x_3=0 \end{cases}\)

The variable \(x_3\) is free, so write:

\(\begin{cases} x_1=-4x_3\\ x_2=+3x_3 \end{cases}\)

And the solution is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} x_1\\x_2\\x_3\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-4x_3\\3x_3\\x_3\end{bmatrix}=x_3 \begin{bmatrix}-4\\3\\1\end{bmatrix}\)

10.

The reduced echelon form of \(A\) is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1&3&0&-4\\ 0&0&0&0 \end{bmatrix}\)

The top row gives:

\(x_1+3x_2-4x_4=0\)

Or

\(x_1=-3x_2+4x_4\)

Notice: there are 3 free variables (not 2). The variable \(x_3\) is free as well as \(x_2\) and \(x_4\).

You can think of

\(x_1=-3x_2+4x_4\)

as

\(x_1=-3x_2+0x_3+4x_4\).

The solution is:

\(\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-3x_2+4x_4\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4\end{bmatrix}=x_2\begin{bmatrix}-3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}+x_3\begin{bmatrix}0\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}+x_4\begin{bmatrix}4\\0\\0\\1\end{bmatrix}\)

12.

20.

Notice the coefficient matrix is the same as that of question 6. Question 6 is homogeneous, 20 is non-homogeneous.

The reduced echelon form of the augmented matrix is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 4 & -5\\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 3\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}\)

The solution is:

\(\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\x_3\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-4x_3-5\\3x_3+3\\x_3\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-5\\3\\0\end{bmatrix}+x_3\begin{bmatrix}-4\\3\\1\end{bmatrix}\)

Notice, the “direction” vector is the same as in exercise 6. The solution to exercise 6 is a line through the origin. The solution to exercise 20 is a line in the same direction, but no longer through the origin; it has been shifted.
